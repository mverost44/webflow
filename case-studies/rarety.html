CASE STUDY

Rarety

Role
UX Designer
Audience
Stylists, designers and collectors alike who are always in the search for rare and valuable one of a kind pieces that are often discovered in thrift stores.
Summary
An ios app for fashion/design enthusiasts to further aid on the mission of making the fashion industry more eco-friendly by promoting buy/sell/trade options for sustainable one-of-a-kind designs.

The problem.

An accessible marketplace with a focus on promoting sustainability that provides transparency on the brands/designer or stores the shopper is purchasing from.

Solution

After extensive research we gathered a lot of information on mistakes that were made in the past and seeing what current leaders in e-commerce with a focus on sustainability are doing and working on to provide a simpler system that aids in promoting sustainability in fashion for even the simplest consumer. After deep diving on the Good on You app it changed the scope a little as I may have started off to broad. I removed the social network aspect to just a simple profile

Discovery & Research

I discovered that there is a collective focus on ethically sourced and sustainable fashion, which means progress is being made on this wave. A whopping 72% of users shop consignment or vintage when not shopping with brands that are sustainable. Many users cited the websites goodonyou.eco and thegreenlabel.

After concluding the surveys, I took design inspiration from apps Grailed, Poshmark, Depop as they were the most recommended. It seems shoppers liked the aspect of a community-based shopping platforms that allow consumers to buy and sell.

Lauren

Lauren is a former model turned Fashion Stylist who loves finding rare and unique pieces for shoots. Often times she ends up collecting too many things and needs to resell but many of her pieces are niche and won’t resell quickly on typical platforms. She loves to do fashion hauls for her youtube channel and is often gifted clothing that tends to pile up. She uses iPhone, is considered an influencer on ig: @laurendots. Uses the app good on you and is a fan of Farfetch and Depop for shopping.

Motivations

Lauren wants to be able to ethically sell the items she was unable to return from shoots or gifts she’s acquired that are of value.

Goals
  • Would love to resell items she no longer needs at a premium
  • Would like an easier way of finding rare items suitable for her shoots.
  • Gain a sustainability rating.
Frustrations
  • Finding one-of-a-kind items that are ethically sourced or deadstock.
  • Finding buyers that actually are interested in the pieces she has to sell.
  • Reducing waste and overstock.

Caio

Caio is a 33-year-old Brazilian fashion designer based between New York City and Sao Paulo Brazil. He started his brand off by repurposing thrift finds and making original pieces from them. Sustainability and benign aware of his carbon footprint have always been very important to him. He’s a vegan, does yoga, also surprisingly owns an iPhone.

Motivations

Caio started an eco-conscious fashion brand that primarily runs via social media based in Brazil. He wishes there was a platform he can not only promote his products to an audience that cares but also sell and provide verification of sustainability practices.

Goals
  • Caio wants to target an eco-conscious community of consumers that will appreciate his brand and build a global brand.
  • He wants to sell through his current collection so he can begin production.
  • He needs to gain sustainability verification to gain trust from potential consumers.
Frustrations
  • Getting his brand listed on a marketplace that cares.
  • Avoiding overstock.
  • Building his brand’s reputation.
